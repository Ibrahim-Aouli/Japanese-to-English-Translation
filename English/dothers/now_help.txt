--------------------------------------------------End--
Overdrives
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Beneath each character's HP and MP meters is an
Overdrive gauge. When it fully charges, the character
can perform a special attack called an Overdrive. In
the command window, press {Direcional Left} to access Overdrives.

By default, the Overdrive gauge charges whenever a
character takes damage. This setting can be changed
later in the game.
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
The CTB Window
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
This window shows the turn order for all
enemies and allies involved in a battle.
The party member or enemy currently
ready to act appears at the top of the
list. Use {u0B:21} and {u0B:22} to scroll up and down
the list, which can display up to 16 turns
at a time. 
Letters like A, B, and C represent
enemies. Check the letter displayed
beside an enemy's name when it's
targeted with the cursor.
Turn order is arranged as if everyone
will use the "Attack" command next.
Different commands shift a character's
next turn up or down the queue.
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Trigger Commands
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
In certain battles, characters will be able to use
special commands. An indicator like the one shown
above will appear above the command window.
Press {Direcional Left} to access Trigger Commands.
Examples of Trigger Commands include the following:

Pincer Attack: Change position in battle.
Talk: Talk to an enemy.
Pull back: Maneuver a vehicle while fighting.{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Change Weapon/Armor{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Press {Direcional Right} during battle to
access the commands {OCyan}Weapon{White}
and {OCyan}Armor{White}, which allow
characters to change
equipment.
Equipped items are denoted
by a mark on the upper left
corner of their icons.
A window on the right displays
the selected item's abilities.
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Defend{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
When you want a character to pass up
his turn in battle, press {Button Triangle} to have
him assume a defensive stance.

Characters defending like this take only
half damage from physical attacks, but
still take full damage from magical
attacks.

This {Button Triangle} defense cannot be used
in certain battles.
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Switch{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Press {u0B:20} during battle to
switch a frontline character
with one in reserve.

The character who enters the
battle can act right away, so
even if you accidentally bring
in the wrong character, you
can immediately switch him
with someone else.
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Damage Indicators{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
In battle, white numbers appear above
combatants to indicate the amount of
damage inflicted on them by attacks.
Green numbers indicate HP recovery.

"MP" precedes any numbers indicating
MP loss or recovery.

"MISS" indicates a failed attack or
failure to inflict a status ailment.

"IMMUNE" indicates resistance to a
status ailment. When facing an attack
capable of inflicting multiple status
ailments, the target must resist them
all in order for "IMMUNE" to appear.
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
The Info Window{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
The auto-ability {OCyan}Sensor{White}, found in certain weapons, reveals
information on enemies during battle. The information
window at the upper left corner of the screen displays
the target's current HP/MP, status, and elemental affinity.
There are four affinity levels: weakness, half damage,
immunity, and absorption. On the chart shown, fire = red,
lightning = yellow, water = blue, ice = white. In the example
above, the target is weak against ice, absorbs fire, takes
half damage from lightning and water, and has been slowed.
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Overkill
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
This icon appears when you strike
an enemy down with an extremely
powerful blow.

When {OCyan}Overkill{White} is achieved, more AP
and spheres are earned at the end
of the battle.

To attain Overkill, the finishing blow
must deal a massive amount of
damage to the enemy. To increase
the chances of an Overkill, exploit
the enemy's elemental weaknesses.
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Elements
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
There are four elements: {OCyan}fire{White}, {OCyan}ice{White}, {OCyan}lightning{White}, and
{OCyan}water{White}, manifested in spells like Fire, Blizzard, Thunder,
and Water. Fire and ice oppose each other, as do
lightning and water.

Many enemies are vulnerable to one or more elements,
and suffer 50 percent more damage from attacks of
that element. Some resistant enemies take only half
damage from certain elements, while others take no
damage at all. A few enemies actually absorb elemental
spells and attacks, thereby healing themselves.

Different enemies have different elemental strengths
and weaknesses, so use abilities like Scan and Sensor
to figure them out.
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Status Ailments
{END}
--------------------------------------------------End--
Enemy attacks sometimes inflict status ailments
in addition to normal damage.

A character afflicted with {OCyan}poison{White} suffers damage
every turn, equal to 1/4 of his maximum HP. Cure him
with an Antidote quickly to prevent his being KO'd.

When a character afflicted with {OCyan}darkness{White} attempts
physical attacks, he almost always misses.
Use Eye Drops to restore his eyesight.

A character put to {OCyan}sleep{White} is unable to act. Physical
attacks will wake him, but magic attacks will not.

A character afflicted with {OCyan}silence{White} is unable to cast
magic spells. Cure him with an Echo Screen.
{END}
